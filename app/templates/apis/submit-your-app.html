Trello loves showing off the tools developers have built on the Trello Platform. There are two types of applications we show-case, Power-Ups and Integrations. To have your app added to our listings, follow the steps below.

Power-Ups vs. Integrations

Unsure of the difference between Power-Ups and Integrations? Power-Ups make use of the the Power-Up API and platform. They are given canvas realestate on the core Trello product. Most Power-Ups will also make use of Trello's RESTful API, but it isn't a requirement. Power-Ups are able to be enabled on boards by Trello users without having to leave Trello. Integrations are often add-ons within other apps and tools that interact with Trello via the RESTful API. Integrations also include Chrome extensions and other browser add-ons.

Power-Up Specific Details

Our bar for 3rd party Power-Ups is that they should provide a user experience at the level of Trello itself. They should not do too much, nor too little. They should be free of bugs and awkward user experiences.

We've put together a list of guidelines for building Power-Ups. Be sure to read through them here.

Check out our Brand Guidelines. Power-Ups should be an extension of the Trello experience. We want Trello users to love using your Power-Up as much as they love using Trello! You can also check out our brand assets.

Power-Up Requirements
  • Power-Up must provide a way to disassociate user authorization via the show-settings capability.
  • There should be no JS errors in console.
Power-Ups Hosting

Hosting Power-Ups is the responbility of the creator. Trello does not host Power-Ups. Please ensure that you have highly available, responsible, and scalable hosting for your Power-Up.

Submitting Your App
